Create `.planning/phases/01-feasibility-spike/01-TAPE-PROTOCOL.md` with the following content:

1. **Purpose** section: Ground-truth tape measurements are the external truth that the harness output is compared against. They must be taken with exact anatomical landmark reproducibility across all testers and all sessions. Landmark ambiguity (Pitfall #15) — e.g., "natural waist" vs. "belly button" — produces phantom variance that looks like model error. This protocol standardizes the method.

2. **Equipment** section:
   - Tape measure: cm-marked, non-elastic, fitness/tailor grade, ≤1cm graduation
   - All testers measured by the same person (partner-assisted preferred; self-measurement is allowed only if no partner available but must be flagged in the data with `_taken_by: self`)

3. **Landmark definitions** (ISO 8559-1:2017 sourced) — define each of the three gate measurements:

   **Waist (Natural Waist)**
   - Anatomical landmark: smallest horizontal circumference between the lower margin of the last rib and the top of the iliac crest — the "natural waist," typically 2–4cm above the navel
   - How to find: tester bends sideways; the fold at the side is the natural waist level. Confirm by feeling for the narrowest point while tester stands upright
   - Tape position: horizontal (parallel to floor), snug but not compressing tissue
   - Breathing instruction: tester breathes out gently, does NOT hold breath during measurement; tape is read at natural rest

   **Hip (Maximum Hip/Buttock Circumference)**
   - Anatomical landmark: maximum horizontal circumference around the hips and buttocks — approximately at the level of the greater trochanters (bony protrusions on sides of hips)
   - How to find: run tape around the hips while the tester stands upright; slide it up/down to find the largest circumference
   - Tape position: horizontal, snug without indenting tissue

   **Chest (Bust/Chest Circumference)**
   - Anatomical landmark: maximum horizontal circumference at the fullest part of the chest, under the arms — typically at axilla level (armpit) for male subjects; at bust-point level for female subjects
   - How to find: tape goes under the arms across the upper back and across the fullest part of the chest
   - Tape position: horizontal, snug without binding

4. **Measurement procedure** section:
   - Tester wears the same clothing as during the scan: tight athletic wear or underwear/sports bra
   - Tester stands upright, arms slightly away from body (not clamped against sides)
   - Take **3 consecutive measurements** per landmark without looking at the previous reading
   - Record all 3 values; compute the average; record the average as `tape_measurements_cm` in the JSONL row
   - Record `_takes_averaged: 3` and `_taken_by: partner_assisted` or `_taken_by: self` in the JSONL row
   - Tape measurements taken at the start of each session (before any scans), so they are not influenced by scan results

5. **Height measurement** section (per Pitfall 7: self-reported height bias):
   - At cohort intake (once per tester, before any sessions): measure tester's height with wall + tape/ruler
   - Record in `Tester.swift` as `heightCm: Double` — NOT self-reported; physically measured
   - Method: tester stands against flat wall without shoes; mark top of head with pencil; measure from floor to mark
   - This value is used as the `userHeight` fallback in `ScaleService` when ARKit tracking is unavailable

6. **Failure modes / protocol violations to document** (not exclude):
   - If measurement is taken by the tester themselves (self-measure): flag in JSONL `_taken_by: self`
   - If tester is wearing loose/baggy clothing for a tape measurement: flag as `clothing_label: loose_baggy` in that row
   - Tape measurements taken more than 30 minutes after the scan session: flag `_delay_minutes: N`
   - These are NOT excluded from the dataset — they become documented failure-mode samples per D-08

7. **Session log checklist** (one per session, printed or on phone notes):
   - [ ] Tape measure cm-marked and non-elastic
   - [ ] Tester in tight clothing or underwear
   - [ ] 3 takes per measurement, values recorded
   - [ ] Height recorded (first session only; re-use for subsequent sessions)
   - [ ] `_taken_by` field completed
